There's a career philosophy called the Tarzan Method, where non-linear career moves reveal new roles, environments, and preferences, shaping a unique career path. These seemingly non-sequential career moves provide you with the skills and experiences you need to increase your long-term potential. My career has never taken a straight line. From high school geometry teacher to Nuclear Engineer to Systems Engineer to Cloud Architect to Customer Success Engineer, now Community Impact Program Manager. This last move may seem disconnected, but arriving felt like a destination.
